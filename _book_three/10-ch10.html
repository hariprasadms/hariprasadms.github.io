Everything hurt.

The surgery.

The stitches.

The exhaustion.

It felt like every part of my body was aching.

I was tired in a way I had never been tired before.

For a few moments, I just lay there, trying to breathe through the pain.

Then suddenly, one thought entered my mind.

My baby.

My heart started racing.

I turned my head toward the nurse.

"Where is she?"

My voice came out weak and shaky.

The nurse smiled.

"Do you want to see your daughter?"

A fresh wave of tears filled my eyes immediately.

I nodded.

I couldn't even speak.

A few moments later, she placed a tiny bundle in my arms.

And that was it.

The moment I saw her, my entire world changed.

I stared at her.

Completely speechless.

Completely overwhelmed.

She was so beautiful.

So unbelievably beautiful that it almost hurt to look at her.

I had spent nine months imagining her face.

Wondering what she would look like.

Whose eyes she would have.

Whose smile she would have.

But nothing could have prepared me for the little girl lying in my arms.

She had soft rosy cheeks.

Tiny pink lips.

Long dark eyelashes that rested against her skin like little feathers.

A few curls of dark hair peeked out from beneath her blanket.

And her face—

God.

Her face was perfect.

I couldn't stop staring.

I didn't want to stop staring.

Tears rolled down my cheeks one after another.

Not because I was sad.

Because I was overwhelmed by how much I already loved her.

The nurse gently adjusted the blanket around her.

But I barely noticed.

My eyes never left my daughter.

Then slowly, she opened her eyes.

And looked at me.

Just for a second.

One tiny second.

But it felt like forever.

The moment our eyes met, something inside me completely broke apart.

I started crying harder.

Because suddenly she wasn't just a baby.

She was my baby.

My daughter.

The little girl I had carried beneath my heart for months.

The little girl I had already worried about.

Dreamed about.

Loved before I had ever seen her face.

"Hi, baby."

My voice trembled.

"Hi."

She blinked sleepily.

As if she had no idea what she had just done to me.

I laughed through my tears.

Then she stretched one tiny hand out of the blanket.

Instinctively, I offered my finger.

Her tiny fingers wrapped around it immediately.

And I completely lost it.

I bent forward as much as I could despite the pain and pressed my lips gently against her forehead.

A tiny forehead.

A tiny nose.

Tiny fingers.

Tiny everything.

And yet somehow, she filled my entire heart.

In that moment, the pain from the surgery disappeared.

Not physically.

It was still there.

But it no longer mattered.

Because every difficult moment had led me here.

To this little girl.

To this miracle sleeping in my arms.

I looked down at her and whispered,

"You're the most beautiful thing I've ever seen."

More tears slipped down my face.

I wasn't embarrassed by them.

I couldn't stop them even if I wanted to.

Because I had never felt a love like this before.

A love so big it scared me.

A love so deep that I knew I would spend the rest of my life protecting it.

The nurse eventually asked if she should take the baby so I could rest.

But I shook my head.

I wasn't ready.

I wanted one more minute.

Then another.

Then another.

I wanted to memorize everything.

The way she breathed.

The way her tiny hand held my finger.

The way her eyelashes fluttered when she dreamed.

I wanted to remember this moment forever.

Because the truth was...

The first time I saw my daughter, I didn't just meet her.

I met the person who would become the greatest love of my life.

And from that very first moment, my heart belonged entirely to her.
