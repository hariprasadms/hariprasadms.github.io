I have never liked New Year's Eve.

People say it is the end of one chapter and the beginning of another.

For me, it was just another date on the calendar.

Another reminder that an entire year had passed, and somehow, I still hadn't learned how to stop hurting.

The television was on in the living room.

I could hear it from my bedroom.

Some special New Year program.

The hosts were laughing louder than necessary, counting down to midnight even though there were still hours left.

I wasn't watching.

I was lying on my bed, staring at my phone, scrolling through Facebook without really looking at anything.

Families.

Friends.

Children.

Vacations.

People writing long captions about gratitude.

I smiled faintly.

If someone had looked at my Facebook profile a year ago, they would have thought I had the perfect life too.

Pictures never told the truth.

I kept scrolling.

Then my thumb stopped.

Krish.

He had uploaded a New Year video.

I laughed before I even played it.

"Still giving speeches to strangers?"

Some things really never changed.

I watched the entire video with a smile I hadn't realized was on my face.

When it ended, I shook my head.

"You've become older," I whispered to myself.

"But you're still the same."

Without thinking much, I opened Messenger.

Our chat was still there.

It looked strangely empty.

Not because there were no messages.

Because there were so few.

Over the years, one of us would randomly message.

A birthday wish.

A festival greeting.

Sometimes just—

Hi.

How have you been?

We never really spoke for long.

Life always came in between.

Sometimes I replied late.

Sometimes he did.

Sometimes the conversation ended after two messages.

Sometimes after ten.

Neither of us complained.

Neither of us asked why.

I scrolled upward.

Years.

Tiny conversations scattered across time.

One message made me stop.

It was from years ago.

Krish: Hi, Radhe. Long time. How are you?

I hadn’t noticed it.

Can I call?

Krish had sent one more message.

No worries. Maybe some other time. Take care.

I slowly came back to the present.

My thumb hovered over the keyboard.

For a few seconds, I simply looked at the blinking cursor.

Then I smiled to myself and typed.

‘‘Hariprasad.”

That is, it, because he knows I am the only one who will message him like that.

I pressed Send.

The message disappeared.

I placed my phone beside me.

Then I walked to the window.

Outside, I could already see fireworks in the distance.

People cheering.

Cars honking.

Somewhere nearby, children were shouting, excited that a new year was about to begin.

I rested my forehead against the cool glass.

"I hope you're happy, Krish," I whispered.

It had been seventeen years since I had seen him.

Life had taken us in completely different directions.

I had no idea what his life looked like now.

Maybe he had everything he had once dreamed of.

Maybe he didn't.

I smiled sadly.

Either way...

I was genuinely happy that somewhere in this world, an old friend still remembered to make New Year videos for strangers.
