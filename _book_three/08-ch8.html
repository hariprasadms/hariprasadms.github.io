The bus rattled along the highway.

I sat by the window, clutching my phone so tightly my fingers hurt.

Outside, trees and buildings blurred together.

I barely noticed them.

All I could think about was Tata.

My grandfather.

The person I loved most in the world.

With my wedding date being so close, I did not know what to do.

He was seriously ill.

My mom told me to come as soon as possible. Soon.

I hated that word.

It sounded too much like a goodbye.

Tears blurred my vision.

I quickly wiped them away.

Then more came.

I couldn't stop them.

What if I was too late?

My phone suddenly rang.

An unknown number.

I stared at it for a second.

Then answered.

"Hello?"

Silence.

For a moment, only breathing.

Then—

"Radhe."

My heart stopped.

I knew that voice instantly.

"Krish?"

Another pause.

"Yeah."

I sat up straight.

Four months.

Four months since the day he had said goodbye.

Four months since I'd seen him.

Four months since we'd spoken.

And somehow hearing his voice hurt and comforted me at the same time.

"How did you get my number?"

"Ranveer."

I laughed weakly through my tears.

Of course.

"Are you okay?"

The question broke me.

Completely.

Because everyone else had been asking about my grandfather.

Nobody had asked about me.

Fresh tears rolled down my cheeks.

"No."

The word came out as a whisper.

"No, I'm not."

His voice softened immediately.

"What happened?"

"My grandfather."

I struggled to speak.

"He's really sick."

Silence.

Not uncomfortable silence.

The kind where someone is listening carefully.

Waiting.

Giving you room to breathe.

"I'm scared."

The confession escaped before I could stop it.

"I'm really scared."

My voice cracked.

"What if—"

I couldn't finish.

Krish didn't let me.

"Hey."

His voice was calm.

Steady.

The same voice that had always made difficult things feel easy"

I took a shaky breath.

"But what if—"

"No."

His voice was firm.

"Calm down. Everything will be alright.”

I pressed my forehead against the window.

Trying to calm down.

Trying to breathe.

Slowly.

Patiently.

And before I realized it, I was talking.

Really talking.

Not about hospitals.

Not about fear.

Not about losing him.

About memories.

About love.

About all the reasons my grandfather mattered.

And somehow, with every story, the panic eased.

Just a little.

By the time the bus entered the next town, my breathing had finally steadied.

The tears had stopped.

And for the first time all day, I felt like I could think clearly.

There was a long silence.

Then—

"Better?"

Krish asked quietly.

I nodded before realizing he couldn't see me.

"Yeah."

My voice was small.

"But yeah."

Another silence.

Then a soft exhale.

Almost like he'd been worried too.

"Good."

I looked out the window.

At the road ahead.

At the town growing closer.

"Thank you."

The words came out automatically.

Because I didn't know what else to say.

Krish was quiet for a moment.

Then—

"Anytime, Radhe."

My throat tightened.

I closed my eyes.

Holding the phone a little closer.

Neither of us spoke.

But for the first time since the call came from home, I wasn't scared of the silence.

Because Krish was there.

And somehow, that was enough.
