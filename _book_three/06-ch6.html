I never thought that Krish would love me. Now it feels like this is all my fault. I should have told him before. I don’t know what to do. We both have found a new normal. Sitting on a bench in a park for hours and just staring. Eating ice creams. Him dropping me every day on his bike and just being there for me. Even though he knows that I am already engaged and never love him the way he does.

***

The park was quieter than usual.

The evening sun painted everything gold, and the lake shimmered beneath the fading light.

It should have felt peaceful.

Krish sat beside me on the bench.

Neither of us spoke.

"Radhe."

"Hmm?"

"I love you." He spoke.

I didn’t reply.

Not because I don’t care or I don’t want to.

But I don’t know what to say.

***

It has been heavily raining from the past one hour. I am sitting in my balcony watching the rain, when I get a call. It is my mom.

"Amma?"

"Radhe!"

My mother's voice sounded unusually excited.

Almost breathless.

"Are you busy?"

"A little. Why?"

"Leave."

I blinked.

"What?"

"Leave your job and come home."

I laughed.

"What are you talking about?"

"I'm serious."

In the background, I could hear relatives talking.

Someone laughing.

The sound of utensils clattering.

The house sounded alive.

Chaotic.

Busy.

And suddenly a knot formed in my stomach.

Because I knew exactly what that meant.

Wedding preparations.

"Amma..."

My voice became quieter.

"You need to come home."

My mother sounded emotional now.

"There is so much work."

"The invitations are ready."

"The caterer needs final confirmation."

"We still have to buy jewellery."

"And your aunt wants to see sarees."

I closed my eyes.

Every word felt like another step toward something inevitable.

Something permanent.

Your father and I were talking."

My mother continued happily.

"We don't want you worrying about office work now."

"We want you here."

"With us."

A long silence followed.

"Radhe?"

"Yeah."

My voice sounded distant.

"When can you resign?"

The question hit harder than it should have.

Resign.

Such a simple word.

Yet it felt like someone had reached inside my chest.

Because suddenly this wasn't just about getting married.

It was about leaving.

Leaving this city.

Leaving this office.

Leaving the life, I'd built for myself.

Leaving Krish.

The thought appeared instantly.

Without permission.

Without warning.

For me he was my best friend, the only person who was there for me in the worst of the situations and I know I will miss him.
