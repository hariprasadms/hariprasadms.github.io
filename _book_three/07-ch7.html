The city lights blurred as I rode through the evening traffic.

Radhe sat behind me on the bike.

Quiet.

Unusually quiet.

Then I frowned.

Because something else had caught my attention.

Something that always did.

Every single time she sat behind me. She never held onto me.

Never.

Instead, she always held the metal grab rail behind her seat.

As if touching me would somehow be a crime.

I had noticed it months ago.

And today, for some reason, it bothered me more than usual.

I stopped at a red light.

The road ahead glowed with brake lights.

Without turning around, I spoke.

"Can I ask you something?"

"Hmm?"

"Why do you always hold the seat?"

Silence.

"What?"

I laughed softly.

"The seat."

"The rail."

"The bike."

"Anything except me."

She didn’t answer.

"Do I look that unreliable?"

"No."

"Then why?"

The light turned green.

I accelerated. The ride continued in silence.

And for the first time, I wished she would hold onto me.

Not because of the traffic.

Not because of safety.

But because I wanted to know what it felt like.

Just once.

To pretend she was mine.

***

The road stretched endlessly ahead of us.

The wind rushed past.

The city lights blurred into streaks of gold and white.

And Radhe was still holding the seat behind her.

Not me.

Then suddenly—

"Krish."

Something in her voice made my stomach tighten.

"Hmm?"

"I have to tell you something."

I slowed the bike slightly.

"What happened?"

No answer.

Only silence.

Then—

"I'm leaving."

For a second, I thought I had heard her wrong.

"What?"

"I'm leaving."

Her voice broke.

I pulled the bike over immediately.

The engine died.

The world became still.

Slowly, I turned around.

My chest tightened.

"What do you mean?"

She looked away.

Unable to meet my eyes.

"Amma called."

I already knew.

Before she finished.

Somehow, I knew.

"I have to go back to Hyderabad."

The words hit me like a punch.

"My wedding preparations have started."

I stared at her.

She continued speaking.

Because if she stopped, she might not be able to start again.

"I'm resigning."

I looked down.

Unable to look at her anymore.

Because tears had already started forming.

And I refused to cry.

Not in front of her.

Not now.

"Krish..."

I couldn't answer.

What was I supposed to say?

Congratulations?

I'm happy for you?

I'll miss you?

None of it felt big enough.

None of it felt true enough.

A tear slipped down my cheek.

Then another.

I quickly wiped them away.

Failed.

Because once they started, they wouldn't stop.

"Krish..."

Her voice shattered.

And that somehow made everything worse.

The woman I loved was sitting right in front of me.

And she was leaving.

For someone else.

For a life that didn't include me.

For a future where I existed only as a memory.

I closed my eyes.

Trying desperately to breathe.

Trying desperately to hold myself together.

Then I looked at her.

Really looked at her.

The woman who had unknowingly become the centre of my world. So, for the last time—

Just once—

I let myself be honest.

"I love you."

Radhe froze.

Fresh tears spilled down her face.

And suddenly I knew.

There would never be another chance.

No more waiting.

No more hoping.

No more pretending.

For a long moment neither of us moved.

Neither of us spoke.

The night wrapped itself around us.

Silent.

Heartbreaking.

Final.
